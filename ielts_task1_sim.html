The provided charts illustrate the weekly expenditure of families in Country X across four categories in 2010 and 2020, alongside the distribution of transport modes utilized by the population in 2020. Overall, family spending increased in all categories except leisure over the decade, with housing and food remaining the most significant expenses. Additionally, cars were the dominant mode of transportation in 2020. Looking at the bar chart, weekly spending on housing experienced the most dramatic rise, surging from $250 in 2010 to $400 in 2020. Expenditure on food also saw a notable increase, growing from $200 to $300. Transport costs climbed more moderately. Conversely, spending on leisure was the only category to decline, dropping to $100 a decade later. Regarding the pie chart, the vast majority of commuters relied on cars in 2020, accounting for 60% of total transport use. Public transport constituted the second most popular method at 25%, while cycling and walking made up a minority, representing 10% and 5% respectively.

The line graph depicts the annual number of visitors to three distinct museums in City Y from 2015 to 2020, while the doughnut chart displays visitor satisfaction levels for the year 2020. Overall, the Museum of Art and the Science Museum experienced an upward trend in visitor numbers, whereas the Museum of History saw a gradual decline. Furthermore, the overwhelming majority of visitors reported being satisfied with their museum experience in 2020. In terms of visitor numbers, the Museum of History began as the most popular attraction in 2015 with 50,000 visitors. However, this figure steadily decreased to 35,000 by 2020. In contrast, the Museum of Art showed a significant surge, starting at 30,000 and doubling to reach a peak of 60,000 visitors by the end of the period. The Science Museum also grew consistently, climbing from 20,000 to 30,000. Turning to visitor satisfaction in 2020, the doughnut chart reveals that more than half of the respondents (55%) were 'Very Satisfied'. A further 30% were 'Satisfied', meaning that a combined 85% had a positive experience. Only a small fraction (15%) expressed dissatisfaction.
