Will vs Going To

Master the future tense in 3 steps: Read the theory, Write your essay, and Speak like a pro.

1. Understanding the Future

lightbulb Theory Overview

Understanding the future in English can be tricky. Both Will and Going to refer to the future, but we use them in different situations.

Going To

  • Prior Plans: Decisions made before the moment of speaking.
    "I am going to visit Madrid next year." (I already bought the tickets).
  • Evidence: Predictions based on what we can see right now.
    "Look at those dark clouds; it is going to rain."

Will

  • Spontaneous Decisions: Made at the exact moment of speaking.
    "I forgot the milk! I will go to the store now."
  • Promises & Offers:
    "I will always love you." / "I will carry that bag for you."
  • General Predictions: Based on opinion, not evidence.
    "I think cars will fly in 2050."

2. Put it into Practice

edit Writing Task

Write a short essay (minimum 100 words) about your plans for your next vacation AND a spontaneous decision or promise you might make during the trip. Use both "Going to" and "Will".

Vocabulary you must use:

ticket hotel explore suddenly promise definitely

Example:

"Next summer, I am going to visit Rome. I have already bought the plane ticket and booked a nice hotel in the city center. I am going to explore the Colosseum... Suddenly, if I see a great Italian restaurant, I will stop to eat pizza. I promise I will take many photos..."
