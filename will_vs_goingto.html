Future Forms

Mastering "Will" vs. "Going to" through Reading, Writing, and Speaking.

The Future of EcoTech: A Vision of Tomorrow

Welcome to the annual EcoTech conference! Today, I am going to share our company's incredible vision for the future. As many of you already know, we are going to launch our highly anticipated solar-powered car, the 'SunRider', next month. We have been planning this release for over three years, and our engineering team is putting the final touches on the design as we speak.

Our CEO, Sarah Jenkins, isn't here with us today because she is going to meet with international investors in Tokyo this evening—a meeting that was scheduled months ago to expand our global reach.

Suddenly, a journalist from the front row raises his hand and shouts, "What about the battery life? People say it drains too fast in cold weather!"

The speaker is taken by surprise but quickly smiles. "That is an excellent question," he replies. "I don't have the exact technical specs in front of me, but I will ask our lead battery engineer to come up to the stage and answer that for you right now."

After the engineer clarifies the battery issue, the speaker continues. "Looking beyond next month, we have bold predictions for the world. We firmly believe that in fifty years, everyone will drive fully electric, self-charging vehicles. The era of fossil fuels will probably end sooner than anyone expects. Of course, we don't have a crystal ball, but our research points strongly in that direction."

Another audience member drops a heavy folder full of papers. The papers scatter all over the floor. The speaker immediately stops and says, "Oh, don't worry, I will help you pick those up!" He steps down from the stage to assist.

Once back on stage, he looks out the large glass windows of the convention center. The sky has turned completely black, and heavy winds are shaking the trees. "Look at those dark clouds outside," he notes. "It is going to rain very heavily soon. Therefore, I promise I will keep the rest of this presentation short so you can all get to your cars safely."

In conclusion, the future is bright for EcoTech. We are going to open three new factories next year, and we will continue to innovate for a greener planet.

Comprehension & Grammar Check

1. Why does the speaker say "I am going to share" and "we are going to launch"?

2. When the journalist asks about the battery life, why does the speaker reply "I will ask our lead engineer"?

3. Which grammar structure is used for the prediction "everyone will drive fully electric vehicles" in fifty years?

4. Why does the speaker say "It is going to rain" instead of "It will rain"?

5. "I promise I will keep the rest of this presentation short". Why is 'will' used here?
