Mastering Just, Already, and Yet

JUST

A very short time ago.
📍 Position: Between 'have/has' and the Verb (V3).
• I have just finished my coffee.
• She has just left the building.
(Usado principalmente en oraciones afirmativas).

ALREADY

Sooner than expected.
📍 Position: Between 'have/has' and the Verb (V3).
• Don't wash the dishes. I have already washed them.
• The train has already arrived.
(Usado en oraciones afirmativas).

YET

Until now / Expectation.
📍 Position: At the END of the sentence.
• I haven't finished my homework yet.
• Have you called your mother yet?
(Usado en oraciones Negativas e Interrogativas).
