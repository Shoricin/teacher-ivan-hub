Past Continuous & Past Simple

La Regla de la Interrupción

Usamos el Past Continuous para una acción larga que estaba en progreso y el Past Simple para una acción corta que la interrumpe.
Generalmente usamos WHEN antes del Past Simple.

bolt

"I was taking a shower when the phone rang."

Acciones de Fondo

Usamos WHILE (mientras) para introducir la acción larga (Past Continuous). Indica que algo estaba sucediendo en ese momento del pasado.

"While I was cooking, my sister was doing her homework."

The Stormy Night

Last Friday, it was raining heavily in the city. While Sarah was reading a book in her living room, her cat was sleeping on the sofa. Suddenly, the lights went out. Sarah was confused. She was looking for a candle when someone knocked on her door. It was her neighbor, Mark. He was carrying a flashlight. While they were talking in the hallway, the power came back on. They laughed and Sarah thanked him for his help.
