Mastering English Tenses

A creative writing workshop. Complete the 3 exercises below to practice Past, Present, and Future tenses in context.

Exercise 1: Narrative Past

The Unexpected Journey

Task: Write a short story (50-80 words) about a trip that went wrong but ended well.
Grammar Focus: Use Past Simple for main events, Past Continuous for background actions, and Past Perfect for things that happened before the trip started.
book Power Vocab
  • Suddenly (De repente)
  • While (Mientras)
  • Had realized (Se había dado cuenta)
  • Fortunately (Afortunadamente)
  • Booked (Reservó)
Tip: "I was sleeping when the phone rang."
Exercise 2: Present & Habits

My Life vs. My Project

Task: Describe your general daily routine versus a specific temporary project you are working on these days.
Grammar Focus: Use Present Simple for habits and facts, and Present Continuous for temporary actions happening now/currently.
bolt Power Vocab
  • Usually (Usualmente)
  • Currently (Actualmente)
  • Nowadays (Hoy en día)
  • Developing (Desarrollando)
  • Consists of (Consiste en)
Tip: "I live in Spain, but I am staying in a hotel."
Exercise 3: Future Vision

The 10-Year Outlook

Task: Predict your life in 2036. What will you be doing? What will you have achieved by then?
Grammar Focus: Use Will/Going to for plans, and Future Perfect (will have done) for completed achievements.
rocket_launch Power Vocab
  • Accomplish (Lograr)
  • By the time (Para cuando...)
  • Hope to (Espero)
  • Career path (Trayectoria)
  • Will have finished (Habré terminado)
Tip: "By next year, I will have saved enough money."
