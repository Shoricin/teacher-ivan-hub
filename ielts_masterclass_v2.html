Proportions & Extremes (Band 9 Model)

Utiliza los botones para identificar la nueva teoría: La regla del "Respectively", el uso de Fracciones, y los Superlativos para marcar extremos en Tablas o Gráficos complejos.

The table and pie chart illustrate the population growth in three cities from 2010 to 2020, and the preferred modes of transport among residents in 2020.

Overall, City A experienced the most significant population increase, while City C recorded the lowest growth. Regarding transportation, cars and public transit were the most popular choices.

Looking at the table in detail, City A had 500,000 residents in 2010, reaching the highest peak of 800,000 by 2020. City B and City C started with 400,000 and 300,000 people, respectively. While City B grew steadily, City C remained relatively stable, showing the smallest change overall.

Turning to the pie chart, cars dominated the transport sector, accounting for 60% of the total, which represents over a half of the population. Public transport constituted a quarter (25%). Meanwhile, cycling and walking were the least preferred options, comprising 10% and 5% respectively. This demonstrates that walking represents only a tiny fraction of commuter habits.
