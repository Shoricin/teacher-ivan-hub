fact_check The 6 OET Assessment Criteria

Your letter will be marked by your teacher against these official OET criteria:

1. Purpose: Is the reason for the letter immediately apparent and clearly expanded?

2. Content: Is all necessary key information included? Is the case accurately represented?

3. Conciseness & Clarity: Are irrelevant details omitted? Is the information effectively summarised?

4. Genre & Style: Is the tone clinical, factual, and polite? Are medical abbreviations used appropriately?

5. Organisation & Layout: Are the paragraphs logical? Is key information highlighted clearly for the reader?

6. Language: Are grammar, vocabulary, spelling, and punctuation accurate?

Case Notes Read Carefully

Assume that today's date is 18 June 2018.


You are a General Practitioner at a local clinic. Ms Anne Hall is a patient of yours.


PATIENT DETAILS

  • Name: Ms Anne Hall
  • DOB: 19.9.1972 (Age 45)
  • Occupation: Secondary school teacher

PRESENTING COMPLAINT

  • 2-week history of gastro-oesophageal reflux
  • Possible stricture

SYMPTOMS & HISTORY

  • Symptoms follow a constant course.
  • Dysphagia for solids.
  • Epigastric pain radiating posteriorly to T12 level.
  • Concomitant 1-2kg weight loss.
  • Problem commenced after an upper respiratory tract infection (URTI) 2 weeks ago.
  • Self-prescribed an over-the-counter (OTC) Chinese herbal product (unknown ingredients) for the URTI.
  • No apparent signs of anxiety.
  • No sensation of a lump.

MEDICAL HISTORY

  • Dyspepsia (2012).
  • Dermatitis (prescribed oral and topical cortisone).
  • Allergic to codeine.

SOCIAL / LIFESTYLE

  • Recently increased coffee consumption.
  • Takes aspirin 2-3 times a month.
  • Ceased smoking 15 years ago.
  • Drinks socially (mainly spirits).
  • Family history of peptic ulcer disease.
  • Current BMI: 28.2

MANAGEMENT / PLAN

  • Recommended patient reduces coffee and alcohol intake.
  • Advised to immediately stop taking the OTC herbal product.
  • Prescribed Pantoprazole 40mg daily.
  • Refer to Gastroenterologist for definitive diagnosis, further investigation, and endoscopy if required.

WRITING TASK

Using the information in the case notes, write a letter of referral to Dr Roberts, a Gastroenterologist, requesting further investigation and an endoscopy if required.


Address the letter to:

Dr Roberts
Gastroenterology Department
City Hospital


In your answer:
- Expand the relevant notes into complete sentences.
- Do not use note form.
- Use letter format.
